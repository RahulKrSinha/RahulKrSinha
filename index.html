About Us

Advanced Clinical Education was first established by Shrikant Chinchalkar, M.Th.O, B.Sc.OT, OT Reg. (On), OTR, CHT in 1989.

Shrikant Chinchalkar has 35 years of teaching and over 40 years of clinical experience in treating upper extremity conditions. He has developed four hand therapy programs at the leading centers in different parts of the country.

The primary mandate of Advanced Clinical Education Inc. is to provide advanced level of education to the therapists practicing for the management of upper extremity conditions.

Our workshops

Enhance clinical skills, thereby enabling you to keep abreast of today’s therapeutic advancements

Help build confidence and skills to immediately implement techniques to provide better patient care

Are in-depth, informative and practical and taught by knowledgeable expert clinicians

Shrikant Chinchalkar

Chief Executive Officer
Shrikant J. Chinchalkar worked as a hand therapist at the Roth-McFarlane Hand & Upper Limb Centre, St. Joseph’s Health Centre in London, Ontario, Canada for 26 years.  He has over 47 years of clinical experience in treating upper extremity conditions and over 36 years of teaching experience. He designed and developed Hand Therapy programs at four major teaching centers in Canada including Roth-McFarlane Hand & Upper Limb Centre in London, Ontario. In addition, he designed WSIB Specialty, Brachial Plexus and Peripheral Nerve Injury, Elbow, Wrist and Hand Rehabilitation, and Hand Therapy Fellowship programs at this center. He worked as a guest lecturer at University of Manitoba, University of Toronto and Western University in Canada. He conducted many instructional courses on upper extremity rehabilitation in over 25 countries in the world and has won many awards for his presentations. He has over 300 presentations to his credit. He was awarded the “Dr. Paul Brand Award of Excellence” by the American Society of Hand Therapists (ASHT), “Award of Leadership” by the Canadian Association of Occupational Therapists (CAOT), “Award of Excellence” by the Sisters of St. Joseph’s Hospital, “Best Educator” by the Western University, “Most Outstanding Prestigious Alumni” by the Nagpur University in India, the “Lifetime Membership Award” by the Canadian Society of Hand Therapists and recently “Excellence in Teaching and Coaching” by the administration of the St. Joseph’s Hospital in 2017. Recently the Hand Therapy fellowship program was named after him. It is now called as “Chinchalkar Hand Therapy Fellowship Program” at the Roth-McFarlane Hand & Upper Limb Center. Recently he was nominated for the Education Committee Chair and also Life Time Achievement Award of the International Federation of Society of Hand Therapists (IFSHT). He authored five book chapters and has published 32 papers and 15 abstracts in numerous peer-reviewed journals related to Upper Extremity Surgery and Therapy.
